Those Who Can’t

Taylor Franson-Thiel

I will teach my daughter how to carry the body of her mother west stepping over the Rockies to bury her beneath the red chest of Utah’s desert. Cup my hands over hers as she

  • smooths oxidized dirt careful to cover the face first
    • if it makes her feel better. She will learn how all daughters of our lineage bury sins of unrepentant mothers. The line of graves, a long ribcage.
